Sector POV — Mining

The Mining Trust Window:
Where the sector picture shifts in 2026

Trust in mining has always been measured in decades — the licence to operate, the generational contract with communities, the slow-moving regulatory dialogue. But the signal is accelerating. This is what the data shows, and where the trust enablers are actually moving.

MissionCTRL Research · May 2026 · 8 min read

The trust landscape in mining has a shape

Every sector has a trust profile. Not a score — a shape. The shape is defined by three drivers: Clarity (how well stakeholders understand what an organisation stands for), Connection (how consistent the experience is across every touchpoint), and Confidence (how clearly stakeholders can see distinctive advantage and proof of delivery).

When all three align, the triangle is equilateral and the trust signal is coherent. When they diverge, the shape distorts — and the distortion tells you exactly where the gap sits.

Mining in 2026 has a distinctive shape. It is not the shape most communications teams expect.

Mining sector trust profile — Q2 2026

Source: TrustOS Mining Sector POV, Q2 2026. Scores indexed 0–100 across stakeholder groups.

Clarity: the sector has largely closed this gap

The sector scores 81 on Clarity — the highest of the three drivers and meaningfully above the cross-sector average. This is the result of a decade of sustained investment in purpose-led narratives, ESG frameworks, and stakeholder communication programmes.

Mining companies have become fluent at articulating what they stand for. The question is whether that articulation is matched by the lived experience of the people it reaches.

What this means for comms leaders

If your organisation is at or above the sector on Clarity, the narrative is landing. The leverage is elsewhere. If you sit materially below 81, you are operating on a different baseline to the sector and the corporate narrative work becomes the highest-priority move.

Connection: the live-edge gap

The sector scores 58 on Connection — the weakest driver by a meaningful margin. This is where the trust shape distorts most visibly.

Connection measures consistency: whether the experience a community liaison officer delivers in the Pilbara matches the story a government affairs director presents in Canberra. Whether the employee onboarding in Johannesburg reflects the same values as the investor deck in London.

In mining, the operational footprint is inherently dispersed. Multiple regions, multiple community contexts, multiple regulatory regimes. The result is that Connection — the coherence of trust delivery — is where the sector leaks most consistently.

Driver distance from sector average — the shape of the gap

Distance from the cross-sector average (67). Connection is the primary leak point across the mining sector, trailing Clarity by 23 points.

Where activism finds its entry point

Connection gaps are not abstract. They are the gaps that activist investors, NGOs, and investigative journalists are trained to find. A community that experiences one version of the brand while investors hear another is a Connection gap made visible.

Confidence: the proof question

The sector scores 62 on Confidence. This is the driver that measures whether stakeholders can point to evidence — outcomes, commitments met, distinctive qualities that make the brand credibly different.

Mining has always struggled with Confidence because the proof cycle is long. A rehabilitation programme takes years. A community investment strategy takes a generation. The challenge is not absence of action but absence of visible, attributable, distinctive action that stakeholders can connect to the brand rather than the sector.

Where the trust enablers are actually leaking

The composite trust score for the Mining sector is 65. That number sits in the mid-range: not in crisis, not in command. The shape tells the more useful story.

Three gap shapes define the sector picture in Q2 2026:

The Clarity-Connection gap: A strong narrative that is not consistently delivered. This is the most common shape across the top 40 mining brands. The corporate story is clear; the stakeholder experience is uneven.

The Connection-Confidence gap: Inconsistent delivery coupled with weak proof. This is where the sector is most vulnerable to external challenge. If the experience varies and the proof is thin, activist exposure finds fertile ground.

The flat-mid shape: All three drivers in the 55–65 range with no standout strength. This is the shape that reads as “adequate” in board packs but leaves the organisation with no distinctive trust position and no natural defence when the cycle turns.

The quarterly signal: what changes from here

Trust in mining does not move in annual cycles. The signal is quarterly — shaped by ESG reporting seasons, community engagement milestones, regulatory consultations and market events.

TrustOS measures this quarterly signal across every stakeholder group. The sector benchmark above is the starting position. What matters is the trajectory: whether Connection is improving quarter-on-quarter, whether Confidence holds when a rehabilitation commitment is tested, whether Clarity sustains when the executive team changes.

The trend is the signal. A single quarter's snapshot is useful for diagnosis. The trend over four quarters is what tells a board whether the trust position is strengthening, holding, or eroding before it shows in the numbers that everyone else watches.

What this means for the next 12 months

Mining enters the second half of 2026 with a trust shape that is readable — the drivers are measurable, the gaps are identifiable, and the levers are known. The question is no longer “do we have a trust problem?” It is “which trust enabler gives us the most leverage, and can we prove it is moving?”

For comms and corporate affairs leaders, the answer is almost always Connection. For investor relations teams, it is Confidence. For the CEO and board, it is the composite — the single number that tracks whether the brand is converting purpose into trust, quarter after quarter.
